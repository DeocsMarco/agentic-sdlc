## 1. Purpose

This work instruction guides the Account Manager and Project Manager through a structured handoff that transfers complete project ownership from pre-sales to execution. The handoff meeting is the PM's first real exposure to the project — it needs to be thorough enough that the PM can confidently lead the project without constantly going back to the AM.

A good handoff takes 2–3 hours of total effort (prep + meeting + follow-up). A bad handoff costs weeks of confusion.

---

## 2. Scope

**Applies To**: Account Manager (AM) and assigned Project Manager (PM)

**When to Use**: After contract signing (SOP Step 3) and PM assignment

**Time Required**: 30–60 minutes AM prep + 60–90 minutes meeting + 30 minutes post-meeting actions

**Responsible**: AM + PM | **Accountable**: AM

---

## 5. Procedure

### Step 4.1: Compile Handoff Package (AM)

**Objective**: Gather everything the PM needs in one place. Do this the day before the meeting.

1. Verify all documents are in the project folder and accessible to the PM:

   | Document | Location Check |
   |----------|---------------|
   | Client RFP (all attachments) | ☐ Accessible |
   | Submitted proposal | ☐ Accessible |
   | Approved strategy (PLAN-STRATEGY-001) | ☐ Accessible |
   | Budget assessment (FORM-BUDGET-ASSESSMENT-001) | ☐ Accessible |
   | Feasibility assessment (FORM-FEASIBILITY-001) | ☐ Accessible |
   | Signed contract | ☐ Accessible |
   | Client communications (emails, notes) | ☐ Accessible |
   | Client clarification Q&A (if any) | ☐ Accessible |

2. Pre-fill TMPL-HANDOFF-001 Sections 1–7 with your knowledge of:
   - Client background and relationship history
   - Project scope and business context
   - Timeline and key dates
   - Budget and commercial terms
   - Team structure and resources
   - Known risks and assumptions
   - Communication plan and client engagement model

3. Identify 3–5 critical topics to emphasize in the meeting — the things that aren't obvious from the documents:
   - Stakeholder personalities and decision-making styles
   - Client hot buttons or sensitivities
   - Promises or commitments made during sales
   - Known risks that need early attention
   - Relationship dynamics (who really makes the decisions)

4. Send pre-filled TMPL-HANDOFF-001 and project folder link to PM at least 24 hours before the meeting so they can review and come with questions.

---

### Step 4.2: Conduct Handoff Meeting

**Objective**: Transfer project knowledge through structured discussion. This is a conversation, not a lecture.

**Duration**: 60–90 minutes
**Attendees**: AM (leads), PM (receives), PM Lead (optional observer)

Walk through these topics using TMPL-HANDOFF-001 as your guide:

#### Client Background & Relationship (10–15 min)
- Who is the client? What's their business?
- New or existing relationship? Past project history?
- Key stakeholders: names, roles, personalities, decision-making styles
- What makes this client happy? What frustrates them?
- Any commitments or expectations set during the sales process

#### Project Scope & Business Context (10–15 min)
- What business problem is this solving? Why does it matter to the client?
- High-level scope: what are we building/delivering?
- Key deliverables and acceptance criteria
- What's explicitly out of scope?
- Success criteria — how will the client judge whether this project succeeded?

#### Timeline, Milestones & Methodology (10 min)
- Project start and end dates
- Key milestones and any hard deadlines
- Selected methodology (Agile/Waterfall/Hybrid) and rationale
- Critical path — what delays would cascade?
- Any timeline concerns flagged during feasibility

#### Budget & Commercial Terms (5–10 min)
- Total contract value and pricing model (fixed/T&M)
- Payment schedule and invoicing responsibility
- Budget breakdown (labor, infrastructure, contingency)
- Profit margin target
- Any budget constraints or sensitivities

#### Team & Resources (5–10 min)
- Internal team structure and allocations
- Any resource gaps or hiring needs
- Client-side team and availability expectations
- Escalation path (PM → Tech Lead/PM Lead → C-Suite)

#### Risks, Assumptions & Constraints (10–15 min)
- Top 3–5 risks with mitigation strategies
- Critical assumptions that could invalidate the plan
- Hard constraints (budget ceiling, regulatory deadlines, technology mandates)
- AM's personal advice on what to watch out for

#### Communication & Engagement (5–10 min)
- How has the AM been communicating with the client? (channel, frequency, tone)
- Recommended communication cadence going forward
- Status reporting expectations
- AM's ongoing role: fully stepping back, or available for escalations?
- When should the PM loop the AM back in?

#### Q&A and Open Items (5–10 min)
- PM asks remaining questions
- Document any open items that need follow-up
- Agree on immediate next steps

---

### Step 4.3: Verify PM Readiness

**Objective**: Confirm the PM absorbed enough to lead independently.

At the end of the meeting, the PM should be able to answer these four questions without referring to notes:

1. **Who is the client and what do they need?**
   - Can the PM explain the client's business and the problem we're solving?

2. **What are we delivering and when?**
   - Can the PM name the key deliverables and critical milestone dates?

3. **What are the top risks and how will we handle them?**
   - Can the PM identify the top 3 risks and their mitigation strategies?

4. **Who are the key stakeholders and how should we engage them?**
   - Can the PM name the client sponsor and explain the communication approach?

**If the PM can answer all four clearly** → Proceed to Step 4.4
**If the PM struggles with any** → Don't fake it. Schedule a 30-minute follow-up within 2 business days to cover the gaps.

---

### Step 4.4: Complete Documentation and Notify

**Objective**: Close out the handoff formally.

1. **Finalize TMPL-HANDOFF-001**:
   - Complete remaining sections (readiness verification, next steps, open items)
   - Obtain three signatures:
     - AM: certifies handoff is complete
     - PM: accepts project ownership
     - PM Lead: verifies handoff quality (if present; otherwise AM + PM signatures suffice)
   - File completed handoff document in project folder

2. **Send joint AM/PM introduction email to client**:
   ```
   Subject: [Project Name] — Introducing Your Project Manager

   Dear [Client Name],

   With the contract signed, I'd like to introduce [PM Name], who will be
   your primary contact and project lead for [Project Name].

   [PM Name] has been fully briefed on your business needs, project scope,
   and timeline. [He/She] will reach out within [X] business days to
   schedule our project kick-off meeting.

   I'll remain available for [describe role: strategic oversight / 
   escalations / stepping back fully], but [PM Name] is your day-to-day
   contact for all project matters.

   Looking forward to a successful project!

   Best regards,
   [AM Name] and [PM Name]
   ```

3. **Notify C-Suite** that handoff is complete:
   - Brief email: "Project [Name] handed off to PM [Name]. Kick-off to be scheduled within [X] days."

4. **PM immediate next steps** (within 5 business days):
   - ☐ Review all handoff documents thoroughly (budget 4–8 hours)
   - ☐ Schedule project kick-off meeting with client
   - ☐ Begin planning phase per SOP-PM-003

5. **AM schedules 30-day check-in** with PM:
   - 30-minute meeting to ensure transition is going smoothly
   - AM remains available for questions during this window

## 8. Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why It Happens | How to Avoid It |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **AM does a data dump instead of a conversation** | Treating it as a presentation | Make it interactive. Ask the PM to summarize each section. Let them ask questions. |
| **Skipping the "soft" context** | Focusing only on documents and deliverables | Stakeholder personalities, client sensitivities, and relationship dynamics are MORE important than the contract terms. The PM can read documents; they can't read people from a PDF. |
| **PM says "yes I understand" when they don't** | Not wanting to look unprepared | Use the 4 readiness questions. They're not a test — they're a safety net. If the PM can't answer, the handoff isn't done yet. |
| **No client introduction** | Assuming the PM will "just reach out" | Joint email from AM + PM sets credibility. The client needs to know who's in charge now. |
| **AM disappears after handoff** | Treating it as "your problem now" | 30-day check-in is mandatory. The AM built the relationship; the PM inherited it. Early support prevents relationship damage. |
| **Not sending materials in advance** | Last-minute scheduling | PM needs 24 hours to review before the meeting. Unprepared PMs ask basic questions that waste meeting time. |

---

## 9. Tips for Success

- **Tell stories, not just facts**: "The CFO is the real decision-maker, not the CTO" is more valuable than any org chart.
- **Highlight what's NOT in the documents**: Verbal commitments, tone of conversations, what the client said off-the-record.
- **Let the PM own the meeting**: After you present each topic, ask "What questions do you have?" Give them space to process.
- **Be honest about risks**: If you oversold something, the PM needs to know now, not 3 weeks into the project.
- **Keep the handoff document alive**: If things change in the first month, update TMPL-HANDOFF-001. It's a living reference, not just a formality.
