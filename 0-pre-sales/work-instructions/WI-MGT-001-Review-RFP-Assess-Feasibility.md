## 1. Purpose

This work instruction guides Account Managers through a single-pass evaluation of a client RFP — from initial receipt through Go/No-Go recommendation. It combines completeness review, strategic fit assessment, multi-dimensional feasibility analysis, and preliminary budget estimation into one streamlined workflow.

The goal is to spend 4–6 focused hours and emerge with a clear, evidence-based recommendation for C-Suite.

---

## 2. Scope

**Applies To**: Account Managers (AM)

**When to Use**: Upon receipt of any new RFP from prospective or existing clients

**Time Required**: 4–6 hours (one sitting or two half-day blocks)

**Responsible**: AM | **Accountable**: C-Suite

---

## 3. Prerequisites

Before starting, ensure you have:

- ☐ Client RFP package (complete with attachments, appendices, Q&A)
- ☐ Company capability profile and services portfolio
- ☐ Current strategic priorities document
- ☐ Resource availability overview (from PM Lead or resource calendar)
- ☐ Standard rate card and historical project cost data
- ☐ Blank FORM-FEASIBILITY-001 and FORM-BUDGET-ASSESSMENT-001

## 5. Procedure

### Step 1.1: Set Up and Organize

**Objective**: Get organized before diving in. 15 minutes well spent here saves hours later.

1. Save RFP package to `RFPs/[Client-Name]/[Date-Received]/`
2. Note the proposal submission deadline — verify you have enough time for the full pre-sales cycle (feasibility + strategy + proposal). If the deadline is less than 10 business days away, flag to C-Suite immediately.
3. Determine if this is a new or existing client. If existing, pull up prior project history.
4. Open FORM-FEASIBILITY-001 and FORM-BUDGET-ASSESSMENT-001 — you'll fill these as you go.
5. Block 4–6 hours on your calendar. Fragmented review leads to missed details.

---

### Step 1.2: Check RFP Completeness

**Objective**: Verify the RFP has enough information to evaluate. Don't waste hours assessing an incomplete document.

Review the RFP against these essentials:

| Element | What to Look For | Status |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| Scope & objectives | Clear project goal, deliverables, boundaries | ☐ Present ☐ Partial ☐ Missing |
| Technical requirements | Platform, integrations, constraints, compliance | ☐ Present ☐ Partial ☐ Missing |
| Timeline | Start date, end date, key milestones | ☐ Present ☐ Partial ☐ Missing |
| Budget | Range, ceiling, or "not to exceed" amount | ☐ Present ☐ Partial ☐ Missing |
| Deliverables & acceptance criteria | Expected outputs, quality standards | ☐ Present ☐ Partial ☐ Missing |
| Submission requirements | Format, method, page limits, evaluation criteria | ☐ Present ☐ Partial ☐ Missing |
| Mandatory requirements | Certifications, insurance, compliance needs | ☐ Present ☐ Partial ☐ Missing |

**If items are missing or unclear** → Go to Step 1.3 (Clarification Loop)
**If RFP is complete** → Skip to Step 1.4

---

### Step 1.3: Client Clarification Loop (If Needed)

**Objective**: Resolve gaps before investing time in full assessment.

1. Compile specific, numbered questions from the completeness check
2. Send formal clarification request to client within 2 business days of receiving the RFP
3. Request response within 3–5 business days
4. Process responses:
   - **All answered** → Proceed to Step 1.4
   - **Partial answers, non-critical gaps** → Note assumptions and proceed
   - **Critical gaps remain** → Send ONE follow-up with a 2-business-day deadline
   - **No response after deadline** → Escalate to C-Suite for No-Go consideration
5. Update FORM-FEASIBILITY-001 with clarification outcomes

**Maximum rounds: 2** (initial + one follow-up). If still unclear after 2 rounds, treat it as a red flag.

---

### Step 1.4: Assess Strategic Fit

**Objective**: Quick gut-check before detailed feasibility. If this doesn't fit strategically, save everyone's time.

Evaluate these five questions (5–10 minutes):

1. **Market alignment** — Does the client/industry fit our target markets? *(Strong / Moderate / Weak)*
2. **Capability match** — Can we deliver the technical requirements with current or near-term skills? *(Yes / Partial / No)*
3. **Size fit** — Is the project the right size for us? Not too small (unprofitable) or too large (risky)? *(Good fit / Stretch / Misfit)*
4. **Strategic value** — Does this build capabilities, reputation, or relationships we want? *(High / Medium / Low)*
5. **Red flags** — Any deal-breakers? (unrealistic timeline, vague scope, compliance we can't meet, known difficult client)

**If strategic fit is Weak across multiple dimensions** → Consider early No-Go. Discuss with C-Suite before proceeding.

Document findings in FORM-FEASIBILITY-001, Strategic Fit section.

---

### Step 1.5: Conduct Feasibility Assessment

**Objective**: Systematic evaluation across four dimensions. Score each 1–5 in FORM-FEASIBILITY-001.

#### Technical Feasibility

- Review required technology stack, integrations, and compliance needs
- Assess current team capabilities — do we have (or can we quickly acquire) the skills?
- Identify capability gaps — can they be closed through training (<2 weeks) or contractors?
- Check for insurmountable technical barriers
- **Score 1–5** in FORM-FEASIBILITY-001 with rationale

#### Resource Feasibility

- Estimate team size needed (roles and allocation %)
- Check availability with PM Lead for the project timeframe
- Assess hiring/contractor needs if gaps exist (factor in 4–8 week hiring timeline)
- **Score 1–5** in FORM-FEASIBILITY-001 with rationale

#### Timeline Feasibility

- Compare client timeline against realistic delivery estimate
- Use historical data from similar projects
- Account for ramp-up, testing, deployment, and 10–20% risk buffer
- Flag if client timeline is significantly shorter than your estimate
- **Score 1–5** in FORM-FEASIBILITY-001 with rationale

#### Budget Feasibility

- Perform rough-order-of-magnitude cost estimate:
  - Total effort hours × blended rate
  - Add infrastructure costs (10–15% of labor)
  - Add contingency (15–30% based on risk)
- Compare to client budget constraints
- Check if target profit margin (20–30%) is achievable
- **Score 1–5** in FORM-FEASIBILITY-001 with rationale

**Auto-stop rule**: If any dimension scores ≤ 2, consult C-Suite before continuing. This is likely a No-Go.

---

### Step 1.6: Complete Budget Estimate

**Objective**: Detailed cost calculation using FORM-BUDGET-ASSESSMENT-001.

Work through the form's sections:

1. **Labor costs** — Break project into phases/epics, estimate hours per role, apply rate card
2. **Infrastructure costs** — Hosting, tools, licenses, third-party services
3. **Base cost** — Labor + Infrastructure
4. **Contingency** — Apply percentage based on risk level (Low: 10–15%, Medium: 15–25%, High: 25–40%)
5. **Total estimated cost** — Base + Contingency
6. **Gap analysis** — Client budget minus total estimated cost (positive = surplus, negative = shortfall)
7. **Pricing direction** — Initial pricing approach (cost-plus, value-based, competitive) and target margin

This feeds directly into Step 2 (Strategy & Proposal) if you get a Go.

---

### Step 1.7: Produce Go/No-Go Recommendation

**Objective**: Synthesize everything into a clear recommendation for C-Suite.

1. Calculate overall feasibility score (average of 4 dimension scores)
2. Apply decision rubric:
   - **4.0–5.0**: Strong Go
   - **3.0–3.9**: Conditional Go (document conditions and mitigation)
   - **2.0–2.9**: Conditional No-Go (high risk, requires strong strategic justification)
   - **1.0–1.9**: Strong No-Go
3. Write recommendation rationale (200–500 words) covering:
   - Why we should or shouldn't pursue this
   - Key strengths and risks
   - Conditions for a Conditional Go
   - Budget viability summary
4. Submit FORM-FEASIBILITY-001 and FORM-BUDGET-ASSESSMENT-001 to C-Suite
5. C-Suite reviews and decides within 2 business days

**If Go** → Proceed to WI-MGT-002 (Strategy & Proposal)
**If No-Go** → Send professional decline letter within 2 business days; file RFP as "Declined" with rationale

## 7. Quality Standards

✅ All four feasibility dimensions scored with specific rationale (not just a number)
✅ Budget estimate includes labor, infrastructure, contingency, and gap analysis
✅ Recommendation rationale is 200–500 words with evidence from assessments
✅ All critical RFP information gaps resolved or documented as assumptions
✅ C-Suite review completed within 2 business days of submission
✅ Clarification loop did not exceed 2 rounds

## 8. Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Why It Happens | How to Avoid It |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Skipping the deadline check** | Excitement about the opportunity | Step 1.1 — check the deadline FIRST. If there's no time, nothing else matters. |
| **Optimism bias in scoring** | Wanting to win the deal | Score based on evidence, not hope. If you're unsure, score lower. |
| **Underestimating contingency** | Assuming best-case scenario | Use the higher end of the contingency range. Projects rarely go perfectly. |
| **Not consulting PM Lead on resources** | Thinking you know availability | Always check. Memory of team availability is unreliable. |
| **Spending 3 days on a bad RFP** | Not doing the strategic fit check early | Step 1.4 is a fast filter. Use it. A weak fit + unclear RFP = No-Go. |
| **Accepting vague scope as "we'll figure it out"** | Client pressure or eagerness | Vague scope is a risk, not an opportunity. Score it honestly in feasibility. |

---

## 9. Tips for Success

- **One pass, one sitting**: This WI is designed to be done in a single focused session. Resist the urge to split it over a week.
- **Use historical data**: Your best estimate comes from past projects, not gut feel. Pull similar project actuals.
- **Be conservative**: Under-promise beats over-commit every time. C-Suite can always push back on a No-Go; they can't undo a bad commitment.
- **Document assumptions**: Every assumption is a potential risk. Write them down now so nobody is surprised later.
- **Talk to people**: 15 minutes with PM Lead (resources) and Tech Lead (technical) is worth more than 2 hours guessing.
