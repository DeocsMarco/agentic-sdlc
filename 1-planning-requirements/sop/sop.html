1. Objective

This procedure establishes the standard process for initiating and planning a new project after contract signing. It covers stakeholder alignment, scope definition, detailed requirements gathering, user story creation, SRS development, technical feasibility assessment, risk management, resource planning, and formal approval — ensuring the project has a solid, validated foundation before entering the Design phase.

2. Scope

In Scope:

  • Kick-off meeting planning and execution
  • Stakeholder identification and alignment
  • Detailed requirements elicitation, documentation, and categorization (functional, non-functional, constraints)
  • User story creation with acceptance criteria and product backlog management
  • User flow creation
  • System Requirements Specification (SRS) development
  • Technical feasibility assessment
  • SMART project objectives and success criteria definition
  • Timeline, milestone, and delivery plan proposal
  • Risk identification, assessment, and mitigation planning
  • Budget estimation and resource allocation
  • Requirements baseline establishment
  • Client requirements sign-off and internal planning approval

    • Out of Scope:

  • Technical architecture decisions (covered in Design phase)
  • Contract negotiation (completed in Pre-sales)
  • Detailed task scheduling (created during Development phase)
  • Ongoing risk monitoring (covered in project execution)

    • Applies To:

  • All new client projects after contract signing
  • Major change requests requiring re-planning
  • Projects transitioning from discovery/pre-sales to execution phase

    6. Procedure

    Step 1: Kickoff Meeting

    Coordinate with client and internal team to schedule and execute the formal project kick-off meeting.

  • Responsible: Project Manager
  • Work Instruction: WI-PMR-001
  • Action:
  • Review handoff documentation and contract for key dates/constraints
  • Identify all required attendees (client stakeholders, internal team leads)
  • Send meeting invite with agenda (TMPL-AGENDA-001) attached — minimum 3 business days notice
  • Conduct kick-off meeting:
  • Introductions and project overview
  • Review project objectives and success criteria
  • Confirm timeline expectations and key milestones
  • Establish communication channels and meeting cadence
  • Define escalation paths for issues
  • Begin initial requirements discussion
  • Document all decisions and action items
  • Distribute meeting notes within 24 hours; track action items to completion
  • Inputs: Project Handoff Document, Signed Contract (TMPL-CONTRACT-001), Project Charter (TMPL-CHARTER-001)
  • Output: Meeting notes with decisions, action items, communication plan, and initial requirements

    • Tip: Record the meeting (with client permission) for team members who couldn't attend.

    Step 2: Requirements Gathering

    Elicit, document, categorize, and prioritize all project requirements, and define SMART project objectives with success criteria.

  • Responsible: Project Manager, Business Analyst
  • Work Instruction: WI-PMR-002
  • Action:
  • Plan requirements gathering activities (interviews, workshops, surveys, observation, document analysis)
  • Conduct stakeholder interviews using FORM-PM-005-001
  • Facilitate requirements workshops with cross-functional teams
  • Review existing client documentation and systems
  • Document functional requirements (what the system must do)
  • Document non-functional requirements with measurable targets (performance, security, scalability, usability, compliance)
  • Categorize and prioritize requirements using MoSCoW framework (Must Haves ≤ 60% of effort)
  • Define SMART project objectives aligned with business case:
  • Establish KPIs, baselines, and targets for each objective
  • Cross-reference objectives to requirements (every Must Have should trace to an objective)
  • Validate completeness: internal review with Tech Lead, Designer, QA Lead
  • Verify with CHECK-REQ-001 (Requirements Gathering Checklist)
  • Inputs: Meeting notes, client documentation, stakeholder access, Project Charter, Business Case
  • Output: Requirements Document, Prioritized Requirements List, Project Objectives Document

    • Go/No-Go — proceed to Step 3 when:

  • ☐ All requirement areas covered, no ambiguous items, client confirms completeness
  • ☐ All objectives pass SMART validation with measurable success criteria
  • ☐ Must Have requirements ≤ 60% of total effort
  • ☐ Internal team reviewed for feasibility; TBD items < 10% with resolution plan

    • Step 3: User Stories & SRS

    Transform requirements into user stories with acceptance criteria and compile the System Requirements Specification.

  • Responsible: Project Manager, Business Analyst
  • Work Instruction: WI-PMR-003
  • Action:

    • Part A — User Stories:

  • Define user personas and roles
  • Transform requirements into user stories (As a… I want… So that…)
  • Define acceptance criteria for each story (Given/When/Then)
  • Estimate story points (optional at this stage)
  • Group stories into epics/features
  • Create prioritized product backlog

    • Part B — SRS:

  • Define system overview and context
  • Document functional requirements with use cases
  • Define non-functional requirements with metrics
  • Document interfaces (user, hardware, software, communication)
  • Define data requirements
  • Identify system integration points
  • Build requirements traceability matrix
  • Compile and peer-review SRS
  • Inputs: Requirements Document, Project Objectives, Non-functional Requirements
  • Output: User Stories, Product Backlog, SRS Document, Traceability Matrix

    • Go/No-Go — proceed to Step 4 when:

  • ☐ All requirements covered by stories; SRS complete
  • ☐ Traceability has no gaps
  • ☐ Peer review passed; CHECK-REQ-002 and CHECK-REQ-003 verified

    • Step 4: Technical Feasibility

    Assess whether all requirements are technically achievable within the project's constraints.

  • Responsible: CTO, Tech Lead
  • Work Instruction: WI-PMR-004
  • Action:
  • Review SRS with technical team
  • Assess technical complexity across all dimensions
  • Identify technology constraints and knowledge gaps
  • Evaluate integration requirements for each external system
  • Assess resource and timeline feasibility
  • Identify technical risks with mitigation plans
  • Document feasibility findings and verdict
  • Verify with CHECK-REQ-004 (Technical Feasibility Checklist)
  • Inputs: SRS Document, User Stories, Team Skills Inventory, Technology Constraints
  • Output: Feasibility Assessment Report, Technical Risk Register

    • Go/No-Go — proceed to Step 5 when:

  • ☐ All requirements assessed as feasible or conditionally feasible with agreed mitigations
  • ☐ Infeasible items revised with client agreement before proceeding

    • Step 5: Risk & Resource Planning

    Identify and mitigate project risks, estimate effort, allocate resources, build the timeline, and calculate the budget.

  • Responsible: Project Manager
  • Work Instruction: WI-PMR-005
  • Action:
  • Conduct risk identification workshop across all categories (technical, resource, schedule, budget, client, external)
  • Score each risk (probability × impact); prioritize by score
  • Develop mitigation strategies for risks scoring ≥ 4; assign risk owners
  • Compile Risk Register (TMPL-RISK-001)
  • Estimate effort per phase and epic; get Tech Lead sign-off on technical estimates
  • Identify required roles and confirm resource availability with managers
  • Build project timeline with milestones, dependencies, and critical path
  • Calculate budget (labor + non-labor + contingency based on risk profile)
  • Compare budget to contract; reconcile or escalate gaps
  • Compile executive summary of all risk and resource planning deliverables
  • Inputs: Requirements Document, SRS, Feasibility Assessment, Project Objectives, Contract, Resource Availability, Historical Data
  • Output: Risk Register, Budget Estimate, Resource Plan, Project Timeline, Budget Reconciliation, Executive Summary

    • Go/No-Go — proceed to Step 6 when:

  • ☐ Risk Register complete with mitigations for all scores ≥ 4
  • ☐ Budget is within contract or over-budget escalation is resolved
  • ☐ Resource availability confirmed (not assumed)
  • ☐ Timeline fits contract dates or gap is flagged and addressed

    • Step 6: Sign-off & Approval

    Secure formal client sign-off on the requirements baseline and internal leadership approval to proceed to Design.

  • Responsible: Project Manager
  • Work Instruction: WI-PMR-006
  • Action:

    • Client Sign-off:

  • Compile requirements package (SRS, user stories, objectives, timeline, assumptions, out-of-scope items)
  • Distribute review package ≥ 3 business days before review meeting
  • Conduct formal review meeting — walk through requirements, feasibility findings, user stories
  • Obtain formal client signature on FORM-PM-008-001 (verbal approval is NOT sufficient)
  • Distribute and archive signed requirements baseline

    • Internal Approval:

  • Compile Planning Package; verify with FORM-PM-009-001 checklist
  • Prepare and deliver leadership presentation (send summary ≥ 1 day in advance)
  • Obtain leadership decision and signature on FORM-PM-009-003
  • Communicate approval to all stakeholders; schedule Design phase kick-off within 3–5 business days
  • Close Planning & Requirements phase: archive documents, baseline requirements, update project status, record lessons learned
  • Inputs: All planning and requirements documents, FORM-PM-008-001, FORM-PM-009-001, CHECK-REQ-005
  • Output: Signed Requirements Sign-off, Planning Approval, Requirements Baseline, Design Phase Kick-off scheduled

    • ⚠️ Warning: Do not proceed to Design phase without both formal client sign-off AND internal leadership approval. Verbal approval is not sufficient for either.
