## 1. Purpose

This work instruction provides step-by-step guidance for planning, conducting, and following up on the project kick-off meeting as defined in Step 1 of the Planning & Requirements SOP (SOP-PMR-001). It ensures consistent meeting execution, stakeholder alignment, and proper capture of decisions and action items that set the foundation for the entire project.

---

## 2. Scope

**Applies To:**
- All new client projects entering the Planning & Requirements phase
- Major change requests requiring a re-planning kick-off
- Projects transitioning from discovery/pre-sales to execution

**Responsible Role:** Project Manager

---

## 5. Procedure Steps

### Step 1.1: Review Handoff Documentation

**Action:** Thoroughly review all handoff materials to prepare yourself for the kick-off.

**How to Execute:**

1. Read the Project Handoff Document end-to-end, noting:
   - Project background and business context
   - Key deliverables and client expectations
   - Known constraints (timeline, budget, technology)
   - Any red flags or concerns raised during pre-sales
2. Review the Signed Contract for:
   - Contracted scope and deliverables
   - Key dates, milestones, and deadlines
   - Payment terms and triggers
   - Penalty/SLA clauses that affect planning
3. Read the Project Charter for:
   - High-level objectives and success criteria
   - Stakeholder list and their roles
   - Assumptions and constraints
4. Note any gaps, ambiguities, or questions to raise during kick-off
5. Complete FORM-PM-004-001 (Pre-Meeting Preparation Checklist) Section 1: Document Review

**Expected Outcome:** PM has full context of the project and a list of clarification questions

**Quality Check:** ✅ All handoff documents reviewed, ✅ Questions/gaps list prepared, ✅ No critical documents missing

---

### Step 1.2: Identify Attendees and Schedule the Meeting

**Action:** Determine who must attend and schedule the meeting with adequate notice.

**How to Execute:**

1. Identify required attendees:
   - **Client side:** Project sponsor, key decision-maker, subject matter experts, IT contact (if technical integration)
   - **Internal side:** PM (you), Account Manager, Tech Lead, Lead Designer (if applicable), QA Lead (optional for large projects)
2. Check availability of critical attendees (client decision-maker and Tech Lead are non-negotiable)
3. Send calendar invite with:
   - Clear subject line: "[Project Name] — Kick-off Meeting"
   - Meeting duration: 60–90 minutes
   - Video call link or physical meeting room
   - Agenda attached (TMPL-AGENDA-001, customized per Step 1.3)
4. Allow minimum **3 business days notice** for client availability
5. If key attendees can't make the date, reschedule — do not proceed without the decision-maker
6. Complete FORM-PM-004-001 Section 2: Attendee & Scheduling

**Expected Outcome:** Meeting scheduled with all critical attendees confirmed

**Quality Check:** ✅ Client decision-maker confirmed, ✅ Minimum 3 business days notice given, ✅ Agenda attached to invite

---

### Step 1.3: Prepare the Meeting Agenda

**Action:** Customize the agenda template to fit the specific project context.

**How to Execute:**

1. Start with TMPL-AGENDA-001 and customize the following sections:
   - **Introductions** (5 min): List all attendees and their roles
   - **Project Overview** (10 min): Summarize scope from charter/contract
   - **Objectives & Success Criteria** (15 min): List known objectives, prepare prompts for discussion
   - **Timeline & Milestones** (10 min): Present any contracted dates
   - **Communication Plan** (10 min): Propose meeting cadence, channels, escalation paths
   - **Initial Requirements Discussion** (20 min): Prepare seed questions based on handoff gaps
   - **Action Items & Next Steps** (10 min): Reserve time for wrap-up
2. Add project-specific topics:
   - Known technical constraints or integration points
   - Client-specific concerns raised during pre-sales
   - Any items flagged in your gap analysis (Step 1.1)
3. Prepare supporting materials:
   - Project Charter summary slide or one-pager
   - Timeline visualization (even if preliminary)
   - Communication channel options (Slack, Teams, email)
4. Test screen sharing and presentation setup if virtual
5. Complete FORM-PM-004-001 Section 3: Agenda & Materials Prep

**Expected Outcome:** Customized agenda with project-specific discussion points and supporting materials ready

**Quality Check:** ✅ Agenda covers all standard sections, ✅ Project-specific items included, ✅ Materials tested/ready

---

### Step 1.4: Conduct the Kick-off Meeting

**Action:** Facilitate the meeting, ensuring all agenda items are covered and decisions are captured.

**How to Execute:**

1. **Open the meeting** (5 min):
   - Welcome attendees, thank the client for their time
   - Round-robin introductions: name, role, involvement in project
   - State the meeting purpose: "Align on project scope, objectives, and how we'll work together"
   - Ask for permission to record (if applicable)
2. **Project Overview** (10 min):
   - Present project background and contracted scope
   - Confirm client's understanding matches yours
   - Note any corrections or additions
3. **Objectives & Success Criteria** (15 min):
   - Present known objectives from the charter
   - Ask: "What does success look like for you in 6 months?"
   - Ask: "What would make this project a failure?"
   - Document agreed objectives and success measures
4. **Timeline & Milestones** (10 min):
   - Present any contracted dates or hard deadlines
   - Identify external dependencies (events, launches, regulatory deadlines)
   - Discuss realistic expectations for first deliverable
5. **Communication Plan** (10 min):
   - Propose weekly status meetings (day/time)
   - Agree on primary communication channel
   - Define escalation path: PM → AM → CEO for internal; PM → Client PM → Client Sponsor
   - Agree on response time expectations
6. **Initial Requirements Discussion** (20 min):
   - Walk through high-level features from contract/charter
   - Ask clarifying questions from your gap list
   - Identify who on client side is the requirements authority
   - Schedule follow-up requirements sessions
7. **Wrap-up** (10 min):
   - Summarize all decisions made
   - Read back action items with owners and due dates
   - Confirm next meeting date
   - Thank everyone
8. Use FORM-PM-004-002 (Meeting Facilitation Checklist) throughout to track coverage

**Expected Outcome:** All agenda items covered, decisions documented, action items assigned, next steps clear

**Quality Check:** ✅ Every agenda item addressed, ✅ All decisions have explicit agreement, ✅ Action items have owners and dates

---

### Step 1.5: Document and Distribute Meeting Outcomes

**Action:** Create formal meeting notes and distribute within 24 hours.

**How to Execute:**

1. Within 4 hours of the meeting, compile meeting notes including:
   - **Attendees:** Who was present (and who was absent)
   - **Decisions Made:** Each decision, who agreed, any conditions
   - **Action Items:** Task, owner, due date, status
   - **Key Discussion Points:** Requirements discussed, concerns raised
   - **Parking Lot:** Items deferred to later sessions
   - **Next Steps:** Upcoming meetings, deliverables expected
2. Review notes for accuracy — cross-reference with recording if available
3. Send meeting notes to all attendees (client and internal) within 24 hours
4. Request confirmation: "Please review and reply if anything needs correction by [date]"
5. File meeting notes in the project documentation repository
6. Update project management tool with action items
7. Complete FORM-PM-004-003 (Post-Meeting Follow-up Checklist)

**Expected Outcome:** Meeting notes distributed, action items tracked, documents archived

**Quality Check:** ✅ Notes sent within 24 hours, ✅ All decisions and action items captured, ✅ Filed in project repository

---

### Step 1.6: Follow Up on Action Items

**Action:** Track and drive completion of all action items from the kick-off.

**How to Execute:**

1. Create action item tracker (or update project management tool) with:
   - Item description
   - Owner (internal or client)
   - Due date
   - Status (Open / In Progress / Complete / Blocked)
2. Send individual reminders to action item owners 2 days before due date
3. Follow up on overdue items within 1 business day
4. For client action items (e.g., providing system access, documentation):
   - Send a polite reminder via email
   - Escalate to Account Manager if blocked after 3 business days
5. Update the action item status weekly until all kick-off items are resolved
6. Flag any blockers that could delay the requirements gathering phase

**Expected Outcome:** All kick-off action items tracked to completion, blockers escalated promptly

**Quality Check:** ✅ No action items remain untracked, ✅ Overdue items escalated, ✅ All items closed before phase ends

## 9. Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Prevention |
|---------|------------|
| Client decision-maker doesn't attend | Confirm attendance directly; reschedule if they can't make it — don't proceed without them |
| Meeting runs over time with no decisions | Timebox each agenda item; use a "parking lot" for off-topic discussions |
| Action items are vague ("We'll look into it") | Insist on specific tasks, owners, and dates before closing each item |
| Meeting notes delayed beyond 24 hours | Block 1 hour after the meeting in your calendar for note writing |
| Recording without permission | Always ask for explicit consent before recording; note it in meeting minutes |
| Too many attendees dilute discussion | Limit to decision-makers and key contributors; share notes with the broader team |
| Skipping the communication plan | Miscommunication later is expensive; always agree on channels and cadence at kick-off |
