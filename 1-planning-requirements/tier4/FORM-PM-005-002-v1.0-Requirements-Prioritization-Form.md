## Purpose

This form guides the prioritization of gathered requirements using the MoSCoW method (Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have). It ensures requirements are categorized with clear rationale, enabling scope management and stakeholder alignment on what is critical vs. optional.

**Completed By**: Project Manager or Business Analyst (with stakeholder input)
**Approver**: Project Sponsor or Client Decision Maker

---

## Instructions

1. Gather all requirements from stakeholder interviews (FORM-PM-005-001) and other requirement sources
2. Schedule prioritization session with key stakeholders (60-90 minutes)
3. For each requirement, discuss and assign MoSCoW category based on criteria below
4. Document rationale for each priority assignment to support future scope decisions
5. Obtain stakeholder approval on final prioritization
6. Use prioritized requirements to guide project planning, timeline, and scope baseline

**Important**: "Must have" should be limited to requirements without which the project cannot succeed. Avoid marking everything as "Must have."

---

## MoSCoW Category Definitions

| Category | Definition | Criteria | Typical % |
|----------|------------|----------|-----------|
| **Must have (M)** | Critical requirements; project fails without them | Legally required, core business function, contract obligation | 60% |
| **Should have (S)** | Important but not vital; workarounds exist | High business value, significant impact if missing | 20% |
| **Could have (C)** | Desirable but not necessary; "nice to have" | Improves user experience, low impact if deferred | 20% |
| **Won't have (W)** | Out of scope for this release; deferred to future | Low priority, time constraint, budget limitation | N/A |

**Prioritization Rule**: If timeline or budget is constrained, deliver M first, then S, then C. W items are explicitly excluded from current scope.
