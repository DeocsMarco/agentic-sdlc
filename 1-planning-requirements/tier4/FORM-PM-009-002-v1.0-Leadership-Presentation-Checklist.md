## Purpose

This checklist guides Project Managers through presenting planning approval requests to leadership, ensuring all required topics are covered in proper sequence and leadership has sufficient information to make informed approval decisions. It provides a structured flow for executive presentations.

**Use this checklist when:**
- Presenting to leadership for project approval (go/no-go decision)
- Planning package compiled (CHECK-PM-004 completed)
- During leadership review meeting

**Time Required**: 30-45 minutes (typical presentation + Q&A)

---

## Prerequisites

Before starting this checklist, ensure you have:

- ☑ CHECK-PM-004 (Planning Package Compilation Checklist) completed
- ☑ Planning package distributed to leadership 48-72 hours before meeting
- ☑ Presentation slides or materials prepared
- ☑ Financial data validated (budget numbers correct)
- ☑ Key stakeholders invited to review meeting

## Checklist Items

### Section 1: Pre-Presentation Setup (5 minutes before meeting)

☐ **Item 1.1**: Arrive early and set up presentation equipment
  - **Expected Outcome**: Laptop connected, slides loading properly, screen visible to all attendees
  - **Verification**: Test slides, animations, and any embedded videos before meeting starts
  - **Notes**: _[Technical setup notes]_

☐ **Item 1.2**: Have backup plan ready (printed handouts or alternate device)
  - **Expected Outcome**: If technology fails, presentation can continue with printouts or backup laptop
  - **Verification**: Backup materials accessible and ready to use
  - **Notes**: _[Backup plan prepared]_

☐ **Item 1.3**: Distribute one-page handout summary (optional but recommended)
  - **Expected Outcome**: Each attendee receives summary sheet with key facts (budget, timeline, risks, approval request)
  - **Verification**: Handout matches presentation content
  - **Notes**: _[Handouts distributed: Yes/No]_

### Section 2: Presentation Opening (5 minutes)

☐ **Item 2.1**: Welcome attendees and confirm everyone can see/hear
  - **Expected Outcome**: Meeting starts on time; all participants acknowledged
  - **Verification**: Technical issues resolved; meeting can proceed
  - **Notes**: _[Start time: ______ | Attendees: ______]_

☐ **Item 2.2**: State purpose and agenda for presentation
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership understands this is an approval request and what will be covered
  - **Verification**: Clear statement: "Today we're seeking approval to proceed with [Project Name] with budget of [Amount] and team of [Size]"
  - **Notes**: _[Approval request stated clearly]_

☐ **Item 2.3**: Set time expectations and Q&A approach
  - **Expected Outcome**: Attendees know total time commitment (30-45 min) and when to ask questions (during or at end)
  - **Verification**: Leadership agrees to timeframe; Q&A approach confirmed
  - **Notes**: _[Time allocated: ______ | Q&A: During/End]_

### Section 3: Business Context & Objectives (5 minutes)

☐ **Item 3.1**: Present business problem or opportunity driving this project
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership understands "why" this project matters to the business
  - **Verification**: Business case clearly articulated; ties to strategic goals or pain points
  - **Notes**: _[Business driver: ______]_

☐ **Item 3.2**: Review project objectives (3-5 measurable goals)
  - **Expected Outcome**: Clear, specific objectives presented (e.g., "Reduce customer onboarding time by 40%", "Migrate 10K users to new platform")
  - **Verification**: Objectives are SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
  - **Notes**: _[Number of objectives: ______]_

☐ **Item 3.3**: Highlight expected business value or ROI
  - **Expected Outcome**: Quantified benefits presented (cost savings, revenue increase, efficiency gains, risk reduction)
  - **Verification**: Business value justifies investment (ROI calculation if applicable)
  - **Notes**: _[Expected ROI or value: ______]_

### Section 4: Scope Overview (5 minutes)

☐ **Item 4.1**: Present high-level scope summary (what will be delivered)
  - **Expected Outcome**: Clear list of major deliverables and features (in-scope items)
  - **Verification**: Scope aligns with signed requirements (FORM-PM-008-001)
  - **Notes**: _[Key deliverables: ______]_

☐ **Item 4.2**: Clarify out-of-scope items (scope boundaries)
  - **Expected Outcome**: Explicit statement of what is NOT included to manage expectations
  - **Verification**: Leadership acknowledges scope boundaries (no surprises later)
  - **Notes**: _[Out-of-scope items communicated]_

☐ **Item 4.3**: Confirm client sign-off on requirements
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership knows client has formally approved requirements (reduces scope risk)
  - **Verification**: Reference signed FORM-PM-008-001 with date
  - **Notes**: _[Requirements signed on: ______]_

### Section 5: Timeline & Milestones (5 minutes)

☐ **Item 5.1**: Present high-level project timeline (Gantt chart or roadmap)
  - **Expected Outcome**: Visual timeline showing phases, milestones, and final delivery date
  - **Verification**: Timeline fits on one slide; major dates clearly visible
  - **Notes**: _[Project duration: ______ weeks/months]_

☐ **Item 5.2**: Highlight critical milestones and dependencies
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership sees key milestones (Design Complete, UAT, Go-Live) and critical dependencies
  - **Verification**: Dependencies explained (e.g., "Delivery depends on client providing data by March 1")
  - **Notes**: _[Critical milestones: ______]_

☐ **Item 5.3**: Confirm delivery date aligns with contract or business need
  - **Expected Outcome**: Proposed timeline meets client expectations and contract commitments
  - **Verification**: No timeline red flags; leadership comfortable with proposed schedule
  - **Notes**: _[Delivery date: ______ | Contract date: ______]_

### Section 6: Budget & Resources (5-7 minutes)

☐ **Item 6.1**: Present total project budget breakdown
  - **Expected Outcome**: Budget shown by category (labor, infrastructure, contingency) with totals
  - **Verification**: Budget numbers match WORK-PM-006; calculations are accurate
  - **Notes**: _[Total budget: $______ | Labor: ___% | Infrastructure: ___% | Contingency: ___%]_

☐ **Item 6.2**: Show budget-to-contract comparison and profit margin
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership sees margin % and understands profitability
  - **Verification**: If margin <5%, rationale explained (strategic project, investment, etc.)
  - **Notes**: _[Margin: _____%]_

☐ **Item 6.3**: Present resource plan (team size and structure)
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership sees FTE requirements by role and phase
  - **Verification**: Resource plan aligns with Resource Manager commitments
  - **Notes**: _[Team size: ____ FTE | Key roles: ______]_

☐ **Item 6.4**: Address any resource constraints or staffing challenges
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership aware of staffing issues (if any) and proposed solutions
  - **Verification**: Resource gaps have mitigation plans (hire, contract, train, etc.)
  - **Notes**: _[Resource constraints: ______]_

### Section 7: Risks & Mitigation (5-7 minutes)

☐ **Item 7.1**: Present top 5-10 risks with risk scores
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership sees highest priority risks (from WORK-PM-002) with Probability × Impact scores
  - **Verification**: Risks are clearly described (not vague); scores justified
  - **Notes**: _[Highest risk score: ____ | Top risk: ______]_

☐ **Item 7.2**: Summarize mitigation strategies for high-priority risks
  - **Expected Outcome**: For each high-priority risk (score ≥6), mitigation plan outlined
  - **Verification**: Mitigation actions are specific and actionable (not "monitor closely")
  - **Notes**: _[Number of risks with mitigation plans: ______]_

☐ **Item 7.3**: Communicate residual risk exposure
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership understands what risks remain even after mitigation
  - **Verification**: Residual risk level is acceptable to leadership (Low/Medium acceptable; High requires discussion)
  - **Notes**: _[Residual risk level: ______]_

### Section 8: Success Criteria & Governance (3-5 minutes)

☐ **Item 8.1**: Define project success criteria
  - **Expected Outcome**: Clear definition of success (on-time, on-budget, functionality delivered, client satisfaction)
  - **Verification**: Success criteria are measurable and agreed
  - **Notes**: _[Success criteria: ______]_

☐ **Item 8.2**: Present communication and governance plan
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership knows how often they'll receive updates and what decisions require their approval
  - **Verification**: Status report frequency and format agreed (weekly email, monthly presentation, etc.)
  - **Notes**: _[Status update frequency: ______]_

☐ **Item 8.3**: Explain change control process
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership understands how scope/budget changes will be managed
  - **Verification**: Change approval thresholds defined (e.g., PM approves <5%, Sponsor approves >5%)
  - **Notes**: _[Change control thresholds: ______]_

### Section 9: Approval Request (2-3 minutes)

☐ **Item 9.1**: Clearly state what approval is being requested
  - **Expected Outcome**: Explicit request: "We are requesting approval to proceed with budget of $X, team of Y FTE, for delivery by [date]"
  - **Verification**: Leadership understands exactly what they are approving
  - **Notes**: _[Approval request: ______]_

☐ **Item 9.2**: Highlight any conditions or dependencies for approval
  - **Expected Outcome**: Any caveats communicated (e.g., "Approval contingent on client providing access by Feb 1")
  - **Verification**: Conditions are realistic and achievable
  - **Notes**: _[Conditions: ______]_

☐ **Item 9.3**: Provide recommendation (Go / No-Go / Conditional)
  - **Expected Outcome**: PM provides professional opinion: "Based on this analysis, I recommend we proceed" or "I recommend we address X risk before proceeding"
  - **Verification**: Recommendation is clear and justified
  - **Notes**: _[Recommendation: Go / No-Go / Conditional]_

### Section 10: Q&A and Discussion (10-15 minutes)

☐ **Item 10.1**: Open floor for leadership questions
  - **Expected Outcome**: All leadership questions answered thoroughly and honestly
  - **Verification**: No question dismissed or avoided; "I don't know" is acceptable if followed by "I'll find out and get back to you"
  - **Notes**: _[Key questions asked: ______]_

☐ **Item 10.2**: Address concerns or objections raised
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership concerns acknowledged and addressed (not argued or minimized)
  - **Verification**: If concern cannot be resolved in meeting, action item created to address it
  - **Notes**: _[Concerns raised: ______]_

☐ **Item 10.3**: Document any leadership feedback or requested changes
  - **Expected Outcome**: All feedback captured (someone taking notes)
  - **Verification**: Feedback documented for follow-up after meeting
  - **Notes**: _[Feedback captured by: ______]_

### Section 11: Decision & Next Steps (5 minutes)

☐ **Item 11.1**: Request formal decision from leadership
  - **Expected Outcome**: Leadership provides decision: Approved / Denied / Deferred (needs more info)
  - **Verification**: Decision is explicit and documented
  - **Notes**: _[Decision: Approved / Denied / Deferred]_

☐ **Item 11.2**: If approved, confirm next steps and timeline
  - **Expected Outcome**: Clear action plan: "We will kick off on [date], begin staffing, and provide first status update on [date]"
  - **Verification**: Next steps agreed and scheduled
  - **Notes**: _[Project start date: ______]_

☐ **Item 11.3**: If deferred or denied, document reasons and required actions
  - **Expected Outcome**: Clear understanding of why project not approved and what needs to change
  - **Verification**: Action items assigned with owners and due dates
  - **Notes**: _[Reasons for deferral: ______]_

☐ **Item 11.4**: Thank leadership for their time and decision
  - **Expected Outcome**: Meeting ends professionally with clear next steps
  - **Verification**: Follow-up email sent within 24 hours with decision and actions
  - **Notes**: _[Meeting end time: ______]_

## Completion Criteria

This checklist is complete when:

- ✅ All presentation sections covered (Business Context, Scope, Timeline, Budget, Resources, Risks, Approval Request)
- ✅ Leadership questions answered
- ✅ Formal decision received (Approved / Denied / Deferred)
- ✅ Next steps documented and communicated
- ✅ If approved, proceed to FORM-PM-009-003 (Approval Documentation Form)

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Presentation runs long (>45 minutes) | Time-box each section; defer detailed questions to follow-up meeting if needed |
| Leadership fixated on one topic (e.g., budget) | Acknowledge concern; suggest separate deep-dive session; don't let it derail entire presentation |
| Decision makers absent from meeting | Reschedule or present to available leaders and get absent leaders' approval via email/call |
| Technical difficulties prevent slides showing | Use backup handouts; walk through key points verbally; reschedule if critical visuals are essential |
| Leadership requests significant changes (scope, budget, timeline) | Acknowledge request; create action plan to revise plan; schedule follow-up approval meeting |
| No clear decision at end of meeting | Politely request decision: "To keep project on track, we need approval decision by [date]. Can we schedule follow-up by then?" |

## Presentation Tips

**Do:**
- ✅ Practice presentation at least once before meeting
- ✅ Anticipate likely questions and prepare answers
- ✅ Bring subject matter experts (Tech Lead, Resource Manager) if needed
- ✅ Be confident but honest (don't oversell or minimize risks)
- ✅ Focus on business value, not technical details (unless asked)
- ✅ Use visuals (charts, graphs) over text-heavy slides

**Don't:**
- ❌ Read slides verbatim (leadership can read)
- ❌ Go into technical weeds unless leadership asks
- ❌ Argue with leadership or get defensive about questions
- ❌ Surprise leadership with information not in planning package
- ❌ Rush through risks or budget (these are critical decision factors)
- ❌ Leave meeting without clear decision or next steps
