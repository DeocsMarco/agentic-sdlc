## Purpose

This template guides the calculation of total project budget including labor costs, infrastructure costs, and contingency reserves. It provides transparent budget buildup with formulas for cost categories and compares calculated budget against contract value to identify budget risks.

**Used By**: Project Manager (with Finance input)
**Time Required**: 60-75 minutes

---

## Methodology Overview

Budget calculation aggregates costs from three major categories: Labor (from WORK-PM-005 resource allocation), Infrastructure (tools, licenses, cloud costs), and Contingency (financial buffer for scope/effort changes). The total is compared to contract value to ensure project profitability.

**Key Principles**:
1. **Labor costs dominate**: Typically 60-80% of total budget
2. **Infrastructure must be forecasted**: Don't forget cloud costs, licenses, third-party services
3. **Contingency is mandatory**: Minimum 5-10% for known-scope projects, 15-20% for uncertain scope
4. **Budget ≤ Contract**: If calculated budget exceeds contract, project is at risk (need scope reduction or contract amendment)

**Steps**:
1. Calculate labor costs by role using WORK-PM-005 effort and hourly rates
2. Estimate infrastructure costs (cloud, licenses, tools, services)
3. Add contingency percentage based on project risk
4. Sum to total budget
5. Compare to contract value and calculate profit margin

### Table 3: Contingency Percentage Guidelines

| Project Characteristics | Contingency % | Rationale |
|------------------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Fixed scope, proven technology, experienced team** | 5-10% | Low uncertainty; minor buffer for small variances |
| **Known scope, some new technology or dependencies** | 10-15% | Moderate uncertainty; standard contingency |
| **Uncertain scope, new technology, or high risk** | 15-25% | High uncertainty; need significant buffer |
| **R&D or exploratory project** | 25-40% | Very high uncertainty; expect significant scope evolution |

**Usage**: Select contingency based on WORK-PM-002 risk assessment. Higher risk = higher contingency.

## Common Budget Calculation Errors & Solutions

| Error | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Labor costs underestimated | Using billing rates instead of cost rates | Use fully-loaded cost rates (salary + benefits + overhead) |
| Infrastructure costs forgotten | Focus only on labor | Systematically review all infrastructure categories (Table 2) |
| No contingency included | "We estimated perfectly" optimism | Always include minimum 5-10% contingency; higher for risky projects |
| Budget doesn't match contract | Estimation errors or scope creep | If over budget, reduce scope or renegotiate contract; never proceed at loss |
| Monthly costs not multiplied by duration | One-time cost vs. recurring cost confusion | Separate one-time (setup) from recurring (monthly); multiply recurring by duration |

## Budget Template Example

### Example: Small Web Application Project

**Labor**: 800 hours × $95/hr avg = $76,000 (68%)
**Infrastructure**:
- Cloud hosting: $500/mo × 6 months = $3,000
- Licenses: $150/mo × 6 months = $900
- Third-party APIs: $200/mo × 6 months = $1,200
- Total Infrastructure: $5,100 (5%)

**Base Budget**: $81,100

**Contingency**: 15% (medium risk) = $12,165 (11%)

**Total Budget**: $93,265

**Contract Value**: $105,000

**Margin**: ($105,000 - $93,265) ÷ $105,000 = **11.2%** ✓ Healthy margin
