## Purpose

This checklist guides the Project Manager through facilitating a project kick-off meeting, ensuring all required agenda items are covered in sequence and key decisions/action items are captured. It provides a real-time facilitation guide to maintain meeting flow and ensure no critical topics are missed.

**Use this checklist when:**
- Conducting the project kick-off meeting (during the meeting itself)
- All participants have joined the meeting
- Prepared agenda and materials are ready (Pre-Meeting Checklist completed)

**Time Required**: 60-90 minutes (typical kick-off meeting duration)

---

## Prerequisites

Before starting this checklist, ensure you have:

- ☑ CHECK-PM-001 (Pre-Meeting Preparation Checklist) completed
- ☑ All attendees present or dial-in link shared
- ☑ Presentation materials and agenda ready
- ☑ Note-taking method prepared (template, recording, or designated scribe)

## Checklist Items

### Section 1: Meeting Opening (10 minutes)

☐ **Item 1.1**: Welcome attendees and confirm everyone can hear/see materials
  - **Expected Outcome**: All participants acknowledged; technical issues resolved; recording started (if applicable)
  - **Verification**: Verbal confirmation from all participants; screen sharing functional
  - **Notes**: _[Record attendance and any technical issues]_

☐ **Item 1.2**: Conduct introductions (name, role, organization)
  - **Expected Outcome**: All participants introduced themselves; client team and internal team clearly identified
  - **Verification**: Names and roles recorded in meeting notes; client decision makers identified
  - **Notes**: _[List attendees with roles and contact info]_

☐ **Item 1.3**: Review meeting agenda and confirm time allocation
  - **Expected Outcome**: Agenda shared on screen or verbally reviewed; attendees agree to agenda and timing
  - **Verification**: No major agenda objections; time expectations set (e.g., "We'll cover scope for 15 min, then timeline...")
  - **Notes**: _[Note any agenda adjustments requested]_

### Section 2: Project Overview (15 minutes)

☐ **Item 2.1**: Present project background and context
  - **Expected Outcome**: Client's business problem or opportunity explained; reason for project clearly articulated
  - **Verification**: Client confirms understanding; team members understand "why" this project exists
  - **Notes**: _[Document key business drivers and context]_

☐ **Item 2.2**: Review and confirm project objectives
  - **Expected Outcome**: 3-5 clear, measurable objectives presented and confirmed by client
  - **Verification**: Client explicitly agrees these are the primary objectives; no missing objectives identified
  - **Notes**: _[Record finalized objectives and any clarifications]_

☐ **Item 2.3**: Define project success criteria
  - **Expected Outcome**: Clear definition of what "project success" looks like; acceptance criteria outlined
  - **Verification**: Client and team aligned on how success will be measured (deliverables, KPIs, satisfaction, etc.)
  - **Notes**: _[Document success criteria and measurement methods]_

### Section 3: Scope & Deliverables (20 minutes)

☐ **Item 3.1**: Present project scope (in-scope items)
  - **Expected Outcome**: All in-scope features, functions, and deliverables clearly listed
  - **Verification**: Client confirms scope completeness; no major in-scope items missing
  - **Notes**: _[List all in-scope deliverables confirmed]_

☐ **Item 3.2**: Clarify out-of-scope items (scope boundaries)
  - **Expected Outcome**: Explicit list of what is NOT included in this project; boundaries understood
  - **Verification**: Client acknowledges out-of-scope items; potential scope creep areas identified
  - **Notes**: _[Document out-of-scope items and any boundary clarifications]_

☐ **Item 3.3**: Review deliverable formats and acceptance process
  - **Expected Outcome**: Deliverable formats explained (documents, software, reports, etc.); acceptance/sign-off process outlined
  - **Verification**: Client understands what will be delivered and how they will review/accept each deliverable
  - **Notes**: _[Record deliverable formats and acceptance workflow]_

☐ **Item 3.4**: Address scope questions and document any scope ambiguities
  - **Expected Outcome**: Open discussion for scope questions; ambiguities documented for resolution
  - **Verification**: All attendees given opportunity to ask scope questions; unclear items recorded as action items
  - **Notes**: _[List scope questions and assigned follow-up owners]_

### Section 4: Timeline & Milestones (15 minutes)

☐ **Item 4.1**: Present high-level project timeline (phases and major milestones)
  - **Expected Outcome**: Timeline displayed showing project phases, key milestones, and final delivery date
  - **Verification**: Client sees timeline; major dates clearly communicated
  - **Notes**: _[Record timeline and milestone dates]_

☐ **Item 4.2**: Highlight critical dependencies and client input requirements
  - **Expected Outcome**: Dependencies identified (e.g., "We need requirements by Feb 1 to start development Feb 15")
  - **Verification**: Client understands when their input is needed; dependencies acknowledged
  - **Notes**: _[Document critical dependencies and client deadlines]_

☐ **Item 4.3**: Confirm key milestone dates with client
  - **Expected Outcome**: Client explicitly confirms milestone dates are acceptable (or raises concerns)
  - **Verification**: Client verbal/written agreement on milestone schedule; conflicts addressed
  - **Notes**: _[Record milestone confirmations or date adjustment requests]_

### Section 5: Team & Roles (10 minutes)

☐ **Item 5.1**: Present project team structure and key roles
  - **Expected Outcome**: Team org chart or roster shared; roles and responsibilities explained
  - **Verification**: Client understands who does what; key contacts identified
  - **Notes**: _[Document team structure and role assignments]_

☐ **Item 5.2**: Identify client-side roles and responsibilities
  - **Expected Outcome**: Client responsibilities outlined (provide requirements, review deliverables, approve milestones, etc.)
  - **Verification**: Client confirms who on their side will fulfill each role (approver, SME, reviewer, etc.)
  - **Notes**: _[Record client team roles and contact assignments]_

☐ **Item 5.3**: Establish escalation path for issues
  - **Expected Outcome**: Escalation hierarchy defined (PM → Director → VP; Client Contact → Client Manager → Client Exec)
  - **Verification**: Both sides understand escalation process and when to escalate
  - **Notes**: _[Document escalation contacts and criteria]_

### Section 6: Communication Plan (10 minutes)

☐ **Item 6.1**: Establish regular meeting cadence
  - **Expected Outcome**: Standing meetings scheduled (weekly status calls, bi-weekly reviews, etc.)
  - **Verification**: Meeting frequency, day/time, and attendees agreed upon
  - **Notes**: _[Record meeting schedule and participants]_

☐ **Item 6.2**: Confirm communication channels and tools
  - **Expected Outcome**: Primary communication methods agreed (email for formal, Slack for quick questions, etc.)
  - **Verification**: Tools confirmed accessible to all parties (no firewall/access issues)
  - **Notes**: _[Document communication tools and protocols]_

☐ **Item 6.3**: Define status reporting format and frequency
  - **Expected Outcome**: Status report format agreed (dashboard, email summary, presentation); delivery frequency set
  - **Verification**: Client expectations set for status updates (weekly email, monthly report, etc.)
  - **Notes**: _[Record status reporting approach and schedule]_

### Section 7: Risks, Assumptions, Constraints (10 minutes)

☐ **Item 7.1**: Review initial risks identified during planning
  - **Expected Outcome**: Top 3-5 risks presented; mitigation strategies outlined
  - **Verification**: Client aware of key risks; comfortable with mitigation approach
  - **Notes**: _[Document risks discussed and client feedback]_

☐ **Item 7.2**: Document key assumptions underlying the plan
  - **Expected Outcome**: Critical assumptions stated (e.g., "Client will provide requirements by X date", "Tech stack is approved")
  - **Verification**: Client confirms assumptions are valid; flags any incorrect assumptions
  - **Notes**: _[List assumptions confirmed or corrected]_

☐ **Item 7.3**: Clarify project constraints (budget, timeline, resources)
  - **Expected Outcome**: Constraints explicitly stated; trade-offs explained (e.g., "Fixed timeline means scope must be flexible")
  - **Verification**: Client understands constraints and accepts trade-offs
  - **Notes**: _[Record constraints and trade-off agreements]_

### Section 8: Next Steps & Action Items (10 minutes)

☐ **Item 8.1**: Review immediate next steps post-kick-off
  - **Expected Outcome**: Next 2-3 weeks of activities outlined (requirements gathering, environment setup, etc.)
  - **Verification**: Team and client understand what happens next and when
  - **Notes**: _[Document immediate next steps and timeline]_

☐ **Item 8.2**: Capture and assign all action items
  - **Expected Outcome**: All action items from meeting documented with owner, due date, and description
  - **Verification**: Each action item has clear owner and deadline; owners acknowledge assignments
  - **Notes**: _[List all action items: Description | Owner | Due Date]_

☐ **Item 8.3**: Schedule follow-up meetings or check-ins
  - **Expected Outcome**: Next meeting(s) scheduled (first status call, requirements session, etc.)
  - **Verification**: Calendar invitations sent for next meetings before call ends
  - **Notes**: _[Record follow-up meeting dates and purposes]_

### Section 9: Meeting Closing (5 minutes)

☐ **Item 9.1**: Invite final questions or clarifications
  - **Expected Outcome**: Open floor for any remaining questions; all attendees satisfied
  - **Verification**: No critical unanswered questions remain; attendees indicate readiness to proceed
  - **Notes**: _[Document final questions and answers]_

☐ **Item 9.2**: Confirm action item summary and next steps
  - **Expected Outcome**: Quick verbal recap of key action items and next meeting date
  - **Verification**: Action items and next steps verbally confirmed
  - **Notes**: _[Record confirmation or any last-minute changes]_

☐ **Item 9.3**: Thank attendees and end meeting professionally
  - **Expected Outcome**: Meeting concluded on time or close to scheduled end; positive closing remarks
  - **Verification**: Recording stopped (if applicable); attendees thanked for participation
  - **Notes**: _[Note actual meeting end time and overall sentiment]_

## Completion Criteria

This checklist is complete when:

- ✅ All agenda items covered (or marked ❌ if not applicable)
- ✅ All attendees introduced and roles clarified
- ✅ Project objectives, scope, timeline confirmed by client
- ✅ Action items documented with owners and due dates
- ✅ Next steps and follow-up meetings scheduled

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Meeting running over time | Skip non-critical items (mark ❌) and schedule follow-up for deferred topics |
| Client raises new scope items during meeting | Acknowledge, document as potential change request, defer detailed discussion to requirements phase |
| Key client decision maker absent | Proceed with available attendees; schedule separate session with decision maker for final confirmations |
| Technical difficulties prevent screen sharing | Verbally walk through agenda; email materials immediately after meeting for review |
| Disagreement on timeline or deliverables | Document disagreement as action item; escalate to sponsors for resolution offline |
| Too many action items captured (10+) | Prioritize top 5 critical items for immediate focus; schedule follow-up to address remaining items |
